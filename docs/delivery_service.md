## Role and Importance of the Registered Delivery Service

### 1. **Electronic Registered Delivery**
   - This service enables the transmission of data between parties by electronic means and provides evidence relating to the handling of the transmitted data.
   - It ensures proof of sending and receiving data, and protects transmitted data against risk of loss, theft, damage, or unauthorized alterations.

### 2. **Legal Certainty**
   - Data sent and received using a qualified electronic registered delivery service enjoys the presumption of integrity, sender authenticity, recipient authenticity, and accuracy of sending/receiving timestamps.
   - Provides legally valid evidence in case of disputes about the transmission of data between parties.

### 3. **Data Integrity and Reliability**
   - Ensures that data transmitted between sender and recipient remains intact and unaltered during transmission.
   - Verifies the identity of both the sender and the recipient before delivery takes place.
   - Creates verifiable proof of sending and receiving with accurate timestamps.

### 4. **Compliance with eIDAS/ETSI Standards**
   - Operates according to Article 43 and 44 of eIDAS, which specify requirements for electronic registered delivery services.
   - Uses advanced electronic signatures or seals of qualified trust service providers to protect the sending and receiving of data.
   - Implements qualified electronic time stamps to secure the date and time of sending and receiving.

#### ETSI EN 319 521: "Electronic Policy and security requirements for Electronic Registered Delivery Service Providers"
- Contains the basic requirements that a QERDS must meet
- Sets specific requirements for user identification and authentication

#### ETSI EN 319 522: "Electronic Registered Delivery Services"
- Focuses on the structure and architecture of a QERDS

## Market Creation Opportunities

### 1. **Cross-Border Transactions**
   - Facilitates secure international communications by ensuring electronic registered deliveries are legally valid across all EU member states.
   - Creates opportunities for businesses to expand into new markets through reliable and legally recognized digital communications.

### 2. **Industry-Specific Applications**
   - Enables specialized registered delivery services for sectors like legal services, healthcare, and financial services where proof of communication is critical.
   - Supports vertical integration of registered delivery into industry-specific workflows such as contract management, official notifications, or regulatory filings.

### 3. **Integration with Other Trust Services**
   - Works in conjunction with other eIDAS trust services such as electronic signatures, seals, and timestamping services.
   - Opens new markets by enabling innovative solutions at the intersection of multiple trust services.

## Components of a delivery service
### Role of a Message Broker Service in a QERDS Solution

The message broker essentially forms the backbone of the QERDS technical architecture, enabling the secure, verifiable, and legally recognized electronic delivery that QERDS solutions must provide under the eIDAS regulation.

### Role of Message Delivery Service
A Message Delivery Service plays a crucial role within the eIDAS framework and Dutch electronic access services, functioning as a key component for secure digital communications between parties.
