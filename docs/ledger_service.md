# Qualified Ledger Service

## Placement in the eIDAS Trust Services Schema

| **Main Trust Service** | **Subservices** |
|--------------------------|------------------|
| **Electronic Ledgers**    | **Qualified electronic ledger service** |

---

## Role and Importance of the Qualified Ledger Service

### 1. **Introduction of Electronic Ledgers as a New Trust Service**
   - This service is part of the eIDAS 2.0 framework, introducing blockchain-based distributed ledger technology as a qualified trust service.
   - It ensures that electronic data can be reliably tracked and verified with immutable records, supporting applications such as financial transactions and supply chain management.

### 2. **Legal Validity**
   - According to Article 45a of eIDAS 2.0, electronic ledgers cannot be denied legal effect or admissibility as evidence in legal proceedings solely based on their electronic form.
   - Qualified electronic ledgers enjoy a presumption of unique and accurate sequential chronological ordering and integrity of their data records.

### 3. **Data Integrity and Reliability**
   - The service ensures that data recorded in the ledger remains intact and unaltered over time.
   - It provides verifiable proof of existence for information using future-proof cryptographic techniques.
   - Ensures unique sequential chronological ordering of data records with any subsequent changes being immediately detectable.

### 4. **Compliance with eIDAS Standards**
   - Qualified electronic ledgers must be created and managed by one or more qualified trust service providers.
   - The service integrates with European Blockchain Service Infrastructure (EBSI), providing a government trust anchor for decentralized digital identities in Europe.
   - Follows eIDAS 2.0's technology-neutral approach, allowing for distribution, crypto agility, and performance improvements compared to existing TrustLists.

## Market Creation Opportunities

### 1. **Cross-Sector Applications**
   - Enables secure and verifiable record-keeping across various sectors, including finance, supply chain management, and public services.
   - Creates opportunities for businesses to develop innovative solutions based on immutable and transparent data records.

### 2. **Integration with Digital Identity Ecosystems**
   - Works seamlessly with the European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDI Wallet) and other eIDAS 2.0 trust services.
   - Opens new markets at the intersection of digital identity and distributed ledger technology, enabling innovative solutions for secure data sharing and verification.

### 3. **Support for EU Regulatory Compliance**
   - Facilitates compliance with various EU regulations, such as the EU Supply Chain Act, by providing a secure and transparent way to track and verify information.
   - Creates opportunities for businesses to develop compliance solutions and services based on the qualified ledger infrastructure.

---

## Summary

The **Qualified Ledger Service** is a significant addition to the eIDAS 2.0 framework, introducing blockchain-based trust services to the European digital landscape. By providing legally recognized, immutable, and chronologically ordered records, this service opens up new possibilities for secure data management and verification across various sectors. The integration with existing infrastructures like EBSI and its potential applications in areas such as supply chain management, digital identity, and regulatory compliance position the Qualified Ledger Service as a key enabler for digital innovation and trust in the EU.
