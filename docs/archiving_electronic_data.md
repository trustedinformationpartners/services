## What is it?
Qualified Electronic Archiving (QeA) is an advanced trust service introduced by the eIDAS 2.0 regulation (Regulation 2024/1183) that ensures the secure, long-term preservation of electronic documents and data with legal validity across the European Union. It represents a significant evolution in digital preservation standards, offering enhanced security and regulatory compliance. Qualified Electronic Archiving is formally defined as "a service ensuring the receipt, storage, retrieval and deletion of electronic data and electronic documents"

## Role and Importance of the Archiving Electronic Data and Documents Service

### 1. **Qualified Electronic Archiving**
   - This service ensures the long-term preservation of electronic data and documents with legal validity.
   - Qualified archiving services are provided by Qualified Trust Service Providers (QTSPs). An example is Docbyte.

### 2. **Legal Certainty**
   - The service provides greater legal certainty and a reversal of the burden of proof for electronic documents.
   - It guarantees the long-term validity of digital signatures and other forms of digital information, ensuring they remain legally admissible.

### 3. **Data Integrity and Reliability**
   - Ensures that archived data remains intact and unaltered over time.
   - Maintains the authenticity, integrity, and readability of documents and data throughout their retention period.
   - Preserves the integrity of digitally signed and sealed documents in line with eIDAS and ETSI 119.511 standards.

### 4. **Compliance with Standards**
   - Operates according to the OAIS model (Open Archival Information System).
   - Follows strict requirements for qualified electronic archiving, including NF 461 "Electronic archiving system" certification.
   - Ensures compliance with national and international regulations, including the upcoming revision of eIDAS.
